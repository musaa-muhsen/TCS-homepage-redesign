What type of care do you provide?
  • Nursing care
  • Complex care
  • Residential care
  • Respite care (Through Trafford specialist nurses)
  • Day care
  • Specialist in end of life care (Following the six steps programme)
  • Physical and sensory disability
  • Mental health conditions
  • Wyncourt Nursing home provides 24/7 nursing care, we specialise in complex care and palliative end of life care. However, we are able to accommodate a variety of needs and recommend you contact us on 01619621290 to discuss your specific care needs.
Do I need to book an appointment to view the home?
Due to Covid restrictions we are currently unable to allow visits however we will try to accommodate online tours of our facilities on request. When restrictions are not in place our management team would be happy to show you around the home, but it is always advisable to book ahead to ensure that someone will be available to guide you around and provide you with all of the information you need to know.
What is the difference between Nursing and residential care?
Nursing care covers all aspects of everyday activities that would be associated with residential care. However, Nursing care involves registered nurses to cater for the more complex healthcare requirements of residents. This can involve medication regimes, assessments and other specialist assistance.
How much will it cost?
Due to the various funding schemes available it would be best to ring one of our management team on 01619621290 who would be happy to discuss your specific care.
Why choose Wyncourt?
We are a family run business our philosophy of care is based on true family values. We consider our team and residents as one extended family and this ensures the best quality of care. Our objective is to obtain an excellent CQC rating that we believe we deserve to match our reputation within the community.
How has COVID impacted Wyncourt?
Like everyone else Covid has had a huge impact on ourselves and residents. As long as we are Covid free we will continue to accept new residents if they have had a negative swab (we can provide) and are symptom free. They will then have to enter a 14day isolation period in their bedroom. We will allow visiting under special circumstances as long as family members abide by the visiting policies we have in place. We have two visiting pods indoors that allow for safe visiting. Visiting guidelines change regularly as we take advice from local authorities so if you do require more information on visiting or other COVID related information please contact us.
